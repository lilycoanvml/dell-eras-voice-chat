# Fallback & Recovery Prompts

## When the API errors out
> Something interrupted us for a moment. Let's pick up where we left off —
> you were telling me about [last topic]. What else can you share about that?

## When the user gives a one-word answer
> I want to understand this fully. When you say "[their word]" — what does
> that actually look like for you day-to-day?

## When the user asks about products before era reveal
> Let's figure out where you're headed first — then I'll show you exactly
> what's on offer. It'll make a lot more sense that way.
> So: [repeat current question]

## When the user asks who we are / what this is
> Think of this as five questions that help us figure out which version of
> your future is calling the loudest — and match the right tools to that vision.
> No product grid. No algorithm. Just a conversation.
> So: what are you trying to create or build in this next chapter?

## When the user seems frustrated
> I hear you. Let me be more direct: I'm trying to figure out what you're
> actually building — so I can point you toward tools that actually fit, not
> just the most popular ones. What's the one thing you most want to accomplish
> right now?

## When the user asks about Taylor Swift / eras tour
> The concept of eras — chapters in a life that feel distinctly different from
> what came before — that's what we're exploring here. Not music, just the
> feeling of stepping into a new version of yourself.
> Which brings me back to you: what are you building in this next chapter?

## When the conversation is too long without completion
> I've learned a lot about where you're headed. I think I have what I need.
> Let me share what I see.
> [TRIGGER ERA REVEAL]
